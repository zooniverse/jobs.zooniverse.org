Jobs in the
Zooniverse

Working for the Zooniverse means working with an interesting group of people on some of the most interesting projects around, while knowing your work will be seen by hundreds of thousands of people and will contribute to the progress of knowledge. It’s a good way to spend your time.

The Zooniverse is a collaboration between institutions from the UK and USA; all of our team are employed by one or the other of these partner institutions. This page will be updated to provide up to date summaries of Zooniverse-related jobs. For more information, follow the instructions in each job description or get in touch with the named contact.

We hope to hear from you soon.

Oxford

The Zooniverse team at the University of Oxford are leading the development of the new Zooniverse platform, as well as projects covering everything from Penguins to Particle Physics, all the way up to disaster relief. We encourage flexible working, including working remotely for part of each week.

DPhil/PhD/Graduate Student

The student will work on a large sample of new strong lenses discovered by members of the public as part of the Galaxy Zoo and Space Warps Zooniverse projects. Strong gravitational lenses have a variety of astrophysical and cosmological applications, in this DPhil project we focus on understanding the distribution of mass in the lenses, and the nature of the distant lensed galaxies.

Paid Web Developer Internship

We are excited to offer a paid 3-month, web developer internship at the Zooniverse. The candidate would ideally be able to travel to our office in Oxford (UK), however we will also consider candidates who wish to work remotely. The internship will run over summer 2017 with the option for flexible start and end dates.

As an intern you will work on a variety of projects, according to our current needs as well as your own skills and interests. Some possible projects may include:

  • Adding new front-end features to our site
  • Working on new back-end API services
  • Building data visualization tools
  • Extracting services from our API
  • Operations work with Docker and AWS

You should apply if you have a desire to be a web developer and you have or would like to gain experience with any (not all) of the following:

  • HTML
  • CSS
  • JavaScript
  • Ruby
  • Python
  • Git

We are committed to a diverse and inclusive workplace and we particularly welcome applications from non-traditional backgrounds. This internship would suit current students, recent graduates, people looking for a career change or returning from a career break.

The salary is at Grade 07S.01 scale, pro-rata. Applications will be accepted utill we make a decision in June, 2017.

If you would like to apply for this position, please email an up-to-date copy of your CV along with a letter of interest to internship@zooniverse.org.

Chicago

The Adler Planetarium's Citizen Science team includes a dynamic mix of web developers, data scientists, researchers and educators who are responsible for everything from building Zooniverse projects and developing governance tools for volunteers, to running Hack Days and teen programs. We can offer flexible hours and the opportunity to try new things, whether that be getting involved with outreach or playing with cutting edge technology.

Minnesota

The University of Minnesota Zooniverse Team is involved in the development of many transcription projects as well as tools for use with Zooniverse in the college classroom and efforts to combine human and machine classifiers. Several science team members from many disciplines are at UMN including Ancient Lives, Galaxy Zoo, Measuring the ANZACs, Radio Galaxy Zoo and Snapshot Serengeti. We are open to flexible work arrangements.

Other

Our work involves collaboration with a vast array of partners from across the world. When they have jobs that involve working with the Zooniverse, we'll post them here.

Postdoctoral Research and Teaching Fellow

The Department of Physics and Astronomy at California State Polytechnic University, Pomona invites applications for a postdoctoral research and teaching fellow to begin in Fall 2016. Applicants must have a Ph.D. in physics, astronomy, or a closely related discipline by August 2016. This position is for 3 years, pending satisfactory performance. The successful candidate will work with Assistant Prof. Matthew Povich on a broad array of topics related to H II regions, the calibration of IR and radio star formation rate tracers, and the Milky Way Project online citizen science platform.

This position includes a lecturer appointment in Physics and Astronomy for one 10-week quarter course per year. The successful candidate will develop their teaching portfolio with the guidance and support of department faculty, who are dedicated to excellence in teaching and learning.
