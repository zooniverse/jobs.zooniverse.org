Jobs in the
Zooniverse

Working for the Zooniverse means working with an interesting group of people on some of the most interesting projects around, while knowing your work will be seen by hundreds of thousands of people and will contribute to the progress of knowledge. It’s a good way to spend your time.

The Zooniverse is a collaboration between institutions from the UK and USA; all of our team are employed by one or the other of these partner institutions. This page will be updated to provide up to date summaries of Zooniverse-related jobs. For more information, follow the instructions in each job description or get in touch with the named contact.

We hope to hear from you soon.

Oxford

The Zooniverse team at the University of Oxford are leading the development of the new Zooniverse platform, as well as projects covering everything from Penguins to Particle Physics, all the way up to disaster relief. We encourage flexible working, including working remotely for part of each week.

Web Developer

The Zooniverse team within University of Oxford's Department of Physics & Astronomy is seeking a developer to build web applications for the Zooniverse.org platform.

We're looking for someone to work on the Zooniverse (Rails) API, building new tools for a variety of projects including working with some of the largest astronomical surveys around. Full details are here here.

Chicago

The Zooniverse team within the Adler Planetarium's Science Engagement Division is a dynamic mix of web developers, designer, researchers, communication specialist, and project leads designing and building the core Zooniverse infrastructure as well as custom features, supporting researchers in using the Zooniverse tools, building partnerships, and communicating with the public. The team nurtures an inclusive culture and supportive work-life balance, a flexible work schedule, and a hybrid (remote and in-person) work environment.

Mobile Developer (Part-time Contractor Position)

The Adler Zooniverse team in Chicago is currently seeking a skilled mobile developer to join our team as a part-time contractor.

As a mobile developer, you will be responsible for enhancing the Zooniverse mobile app, available on both iOS and Android platforms. Your main tasks will include identifying and implementing crucial improvements to the code base and architecture, as well as developing and deploying a robust notification system. Additionally, you will play a vital role in maintaining the code base. This NASA-funded position is for an initial period of 2 years, with potential for extension beyond that. In Year 1, you can expect to work approximately 480 hours spread out over 6 to 9 months. In Year 2, the workload will average around 10 hours per month, totaling approximately 120 hours. Please note that this position is offered on a part-time contractor basis within our team.

Qualifications:

  • Passionate about building really cool and purposeful mobile apps
  • Demonstrated experience developing mobile applications
  • Demonstrated experience with React Native (the language the Zooniverse mobile app is written in)
  • Interest in being in an inclusive environment; we strongly value the diversity of our team

Bonus points if you have experience with:

  • Firebase or other tools/platforms for push notifications
  • Web application frameworks (like Ruby on Rails, Django, etc.)
  • Git and Github (or other version control system)
  • Writing and maintaining automated test suites for code bases
  • RESTful JSON APIs

Salary range: $85-$100 USD/hour.

To apply for the mobile developer position, please submit a cover letter and your CV to trouille@zooniverse.org, with the subject line "Zooniverse mobile dev position."

We encourage applications from developers based in Chicago, as well as those interested in working remotely from anywhere in the United States. Note: we are constrained to U.S.-based developers only.

Application deadline: July 21.
Please note that we will begin reviewing applications on a rolling basis, starting June 27.

Minnesota

The University of Minnesota Zooniverse Team is involved in the development of many Zooniverse projects as well as efforts to combine human and machine classifiers. Several science team members from many disciplines are at UMN including Galaxy Zoo, Measuring the ANZACs, Radio Galaxy Zoo and Snapshot Safari. We are open to flexible work arrangements.
